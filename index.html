A few more things...

1. Dress Code
Semi-formal or cocktail attire. Dresses of any length, suits and ties, no jeans. We are going for a sophisticated, Victorian fairy tale vibe. Stick to darker colours like mahoganies/wine reds, deep emeralds, navy blues, etc. Take inspiration from our venues, the invitations...this website, even. Do not worry about outshining the bride and groom!

2. Food & Drink
We will have catered luncheon style and appetizers, as well as wine, at the Mansion prior to the Conservatory. Please note no food or beverages are allowed inside the Conservatory.

3. Accomodation
We will be suggesting a hotel and possibly a room block in said hotel. Please stay tuned!

4. Plus Ones
Since we do have a very limited number of seats (18, for the matter), we ask that you only bring a plus one if it is indicated on your invitation. Thank you for understanding!

5. A Note on Phones & Photography
We love that you're here for us and dressed up, and we want to encourage you take photos! We will have disposable cameras floating around that we would love for you to snap some pictures with! However, please refrain from having your phones out during the ceremony, and whenever the photographer is around. Good times to take your own phone photos would be at the mansion before the photographer arrives, after the ceremony during couple portraits, and at the after party.

6. Do you guys have a registry?
We know most of you are coming from out of town, and that comes with it's own investment. We're happy enough that you'll be here! Gifts are not required at all. If really you do feel like you'd like to get us something, please find our registry located at this link. Thank you!

Our Lovely Venues

Here are some links to our venues for this event. Please make sure you are familiar with any of their rules! Here's a few quick facts about the wedding venues:

The Stimson-Green Mansion is a English Tudor-style mansion, completed in 1901, and is a Seattle Landmark. We will be hosting the event on the first floor only, which is all original (and absolutely gorgeous).

The Volunteer Park Conservatory was proposed in 1893 and opened its doors in 1912. It was originally modelled after Victorian-style greenhouses in Europe, and is also a Seattle Landmark.

The Pine Box, or 1600 Melrose, was The E.R. Butterworth & Sons Family Funeral Business from 1923 until the early 2000s. Bruce Lee had his funeral service there in 1973! Now, it maintains its neo-classical architecture, but is a beloved local bar.
