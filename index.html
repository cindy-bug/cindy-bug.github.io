More stuff around Seattle...

We know tons of you are coming from out of town, so PLEASE let us know if you have any questions at all! In the meantime...

1. Neighbourly insights:

We'll be in between the downtown and Capitol Hill neighbourhood of Seattle. Capitol Hill's been described as "the heart of the city". Densely populated, very trendy and hip, you'll find no shortage of boutiques and events. Downtown is a bit more business-y, it's cleaner, with mostly high rises and offices. Seattle is mostly a safe city, but I'd recommend against going out alone super early or super late. Don't leave anything obviously valuable in your car :)

2. Getting around:

The local transit is called the Link Light Rail. There are kiosks at every stop at which you can buy tickets. Our suggested hotel will be around a 10 minute walk from the Westlake Center Station (which also includes the Monorail that will bring you to the Space Needle). If you do want to drive in to the city, street parking is free on Sundays (good luck!) There's also the Doppler parking garage (under the Amazon Spheres), which offers free parking on weekends and after 5pm on weekdays (no overnight parking, though!).

3. Things to do:

Pike Place Market (inc. Post Alley & The Gum Wall), Waterfront Park, piers, and the Great Wheel (amazing views of the Olympics on a sunny day). Kerry Park for Rainier views, Discovery Park for if you've got a car and a desire to hike somewhere close by. Washington Park Arboretum and the Seattle Japanese Garden for more nature. Visit the Seattle Art Museum, Seattle Public Library, the MoPop, catch a show at the historic Paramount Theatre. If you make it to the University of Washington, be sure to check out Suzzallo Library. There MIGHT be some cherry blossoms still in bloom at the Quad. Back downtown, The Amazon Spheres can be interesting. Take Bill Spiedel's underground tour in Pioneer Square for some history lessons......

4. Favourite local restaurants:

There are too many to name, but if we had to choose some closest to our venues...Breakfast/coffee: Grumpy Bean, Analog Coffee, Caffe Vita, Piedmont Cafe, Ghost Note Coffee, Portage Bay, Eggslut...throw a rock and you'll hit a coffee shop in this city. Lunch/Dinner: The Cheese Room (an all-time fav), Oddfellows, Biang Biang Noodles, Tamari Bar, Momiji, Harry's Fine Foods, Taylor Shellfish (oysters won't be in season though...). Sweets: Nana's Green Tea, Molly Moon's, Voodoo Doughnuts.

5. Shops & other random little places:

Twice Sold Tales (used books and cats), Elliot Bay Book Company (we like books), Rock Box (Japanese style karaoke, book a room for $9 an hour!), Chophouse Row is a good place to chill with a coffee and a book, throw a rock and hit a thrift/vintage boutique in Capitol Hill.

And so, SO many more...please find more suggestions in the map linked above!! These are just off the top of my head.
